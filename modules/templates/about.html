What is Loadicator?

Loadicator is a professional ship stability calculator designed to analyze and visualize the stability characteristics of vessels through GZ (righting lever) curves. It provides naval architects, ship operators, and marine engineers with instant stability assessments based on loading conditions.

The tool is specifically configured for MV Del Monte but can be adapted to any vessel by uploading appropriate hydrostatic data.

How It Works

1. Input Parameters

Users provide two key parameters:

  • Draft: The depth of the ship below the waterline (in meters or feet)
  • Load: The cargo/load weight (in kilograms)

2. Calculations

The system performs the following calculations:

  • Displacement: Calculated from the input draft using the displacement table
  • KG (Center of Gravity): Estimated based on draft and loading conditions
  • GZ Values: Computed using the formula:

3. Output

The tool generates:

  • Interactive GZ stability curve plot
  • Key stability parameters (max GZ, angle of max GZ, etc.)
  • Detailed table of heel angles vs GZ values
  • Displacement and center of gravity information

Understanding GZ Curves

The GZ curve (also known as the righting lever curve) is fundamental to ship stability analysis. It shows how much righting moment the vessel generates at different angles of heel.

  • Positive GZ: Indicates stability - the ship will right itself
  • Maximum GZ: The peak righting ability of the vessel
  • Zero GZ: The angle beyond which the ship may capsize
  • Area under curve: Represents the total righting energy available
